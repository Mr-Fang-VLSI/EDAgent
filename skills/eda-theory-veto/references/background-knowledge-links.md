# EDA Theory Veto Background Knowledge Links

## Core Sources

Use the following as the professional background basis for veto decisions:
- relevant KB policy locks such as comparison policy, route policy, or stage-governance notes,
- local paper summaries and parsed PDF evidence for method or physics assumptions,
- current empirical summary artifacts from the active project.

## When To Load

Load this background when:
- the proposal changes objective or cost-model assumptions,
- the proposal claims a new physical or algorithmic mechanism,
- the proposal challenges an existing repo policy or accepted KB claim.

## Conflict Rule

If the proposal conflicts with background knowledge:
1. identify the exact KB or paper-derived source,
2. state whether the conflict implies `missing_evidence`, `conditional_risk`, or `hard_contradiction`,
3. if the source itself appears stale or invalidated, request KB `update` or `overturn` follow-up instead of pretending the conflict does not exist.
