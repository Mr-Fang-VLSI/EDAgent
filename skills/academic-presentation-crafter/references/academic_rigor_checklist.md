# Academic Rigor & Content Refinement Checklist

This checklist is designed to be used during **Phase 3** of the academic presentation creation workflow. Its purpose is to ensure that the presentation content is a faithful, accurate, and professional representation of the source research paper.

## 1. Data & Metrics Verification

- [ ] **Exact Values**: Have all data points, performance metrics, and statistics been cross-checked against the original paper? Verify every number.
- [ ] **Ranges and Averages**: When the paper reports a range (e.g., "22–26% improvement") or an average, is the presentation using the exact same format? Do not substitute with a single, simplified number.
- [ ] **Table and Chart Accuracy**: Are the data presented in tables and charts identical to the source? Check for transcription errors, incorrect labels, or misleading visualizations.
- [ ] **Data Normalization**: If data is normalized, is the baseline clearly stated and consistent with the paper? (e.g., "Normalized to VPlace = 1.0").

## 2. Terminology & Acronyms

- [ ] **Precise Terminology**: Is the presentation using the exact terminology from the paper for all key concepts, models, and methods? Avoid synonyms or simplifications that could alter the meaning.
- [ ] **Acronym Definition**: Has every acronym (e.g., VAE, BO, HPWL, SOTA) been defined with its full name upon its first use? The standard format is "Variational Autoencoder (VAE)".
- [ ] **Consistency**: Is the use of acronyms and terms consistent throughout the entire presentation?

## 3. Claims & Contributions

- [ ] **Faithful Representation**: Do the claims made in the presentation accurately reflect the claims in the paper? Do not overstate or embellish the novelty or impact of the work.
- [ ] **Correct Categorization**: Are baseline methods and related works categorized correctly (e.g., geometry-driven, rule-based, performance-driven)? This must match the paper's own classification.
- [ ] **Scope of Contributions**: Are the key takeaways and conclusions a direct summary of the paper's stated contributions? Do not introduce new conclusions not supported by the paper.

## 4. Explanations & Notations

- [ ] **Clarity of Concepts**: Are complex technical concepts, architectures, or workflows explained clearly and concisely, without sacrificing accuracy?
- [ ] **Mathematical Notation**: Are all mathematical variables and equations presented correctly? Ensure that notations (e.g., *z* for latent space) are explained or are clear from the context, matching the paper's usage.
- [ ] **Diagram Labels**: Are all labels and annotations on diagrams, flowcharts, and figures correct and consistent with the paper's descriptions?
